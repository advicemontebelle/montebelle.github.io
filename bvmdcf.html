The Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) brand valuation model includes six elements.

The first of these is segmentation, based on the fact that brands only have value in the context of specific markets, both in terms of product category and geography. The second is market analysis, to understand the competitive conditions. The third is brand business analysis, taking the form of a calculation of the earnings of the branded business. The fourth is brand analysis, to determine what proportion of earnings is attributable to the brand. Fifth is brand risk analysis, as to determine an appropriate discount rate, the risk that performance will deteriorate has to be estimated. Finally, there is brand financial analysis, based on forecasting future cash flows over a five to ten year period, discounting them back to present value. The DCF method has been endorsed by the British Accounting Standards Board (BASB).

The Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) brand valuation model includes six elements:

  • 1. Segmentation: Brands only have value in the context of specific markets, both in terms of product category and geography.
  • 2. Market analysis: To understand the market and competitive conditions.
  • 3. Brand business analysis: A calculation of the earnings of the branded business (economic value added).
  • 4. Brand analysis: To determine what proportion of earnings is attributable to the brand.

Brand Valuation Model: DCF

Valuation

To determine the discount rate, we start with the risk-free borrowing rate for the geographic being evaluated (for this, government bond yield is used). This is then adjusted to incorporate an equity risk premium, reflecting the expected returns for investors. The resulting rate is then adjusted for the risk inherent in the market(s) in which the brand operates and subsequently for the risk profile of the specific brand.

To determine the brand's risk profile, a set of key indicators of brand strength is used. The brand under investigation is scored against a notional ideal brand, which would be close to a risk-free asset: the brand beta score. An average brand, achieving a score of 50 will be assigned the average discount rate for that geographic market. Brand value is then calculated by applying the determined discount rate to the expected future brand cash flows.
