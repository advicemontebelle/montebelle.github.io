This article offers five definitions of the word 'strategy' for brands, the relationship between which vary: strategy as plan, or a set of guidelines to deal with a situation; strategy as ploy, or a plan to outwit rival brands; strategy as pattern, or consistency in corporate behaviour, whether intended or not; strategy as position, or locating an organisation relative to its competitors and within its environment; and strategy as perspective, or the beliefs, norms and values of a brand/organisation.
Five Stragic P's

Five Stragic P's

The word 'strategy' is implicitly defined in different ways. Minzberg presents five definitions:

  • 1. Strategy as plan: A consciously intended course of action, a set of guidelines to deal with a situation. This can be general or very specific.
  • 2. Strategy as ploy: A specific manoeuvre to pre-empt competitive response. This is basically an attempt to outwit rivals in a competitive situation.
  • 3. Strategy as pattern: In a stream of actions, consistency in corporate behaviour, whether intended or not. Plans may go unrealised, while patterns may appear without pre-conception.
  • 4. Strategy as position: Locating an organisation in an environment. This can be defined with respect to a single competitor, a large number of competitors, or with respect to markets or an environment at large.
  • 5. Strategy as perspective: An established way of perceiving the world, not just taking a specific position. This reflects the 'personality' of an organisation. Perspective refers to the shared abstractions and opinions in the minds of involved parties.

While plans and positions can be adapted, perspectives are immutable: once they are established they become difficult to change. The shared beliefs, norms and values may become unconscious in the minds of management and staff, ultimately looking more like a pattern than a plan.

Various relationships exist among the five definitions. They complement each other, and no single definition takes precedence over the others.
